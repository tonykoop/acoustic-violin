# Ode to Joy

**Provenance:** Ludwig van Beethoven, Symphony No. 9 in D minor, Op. 125, 1824.
Public domain (Beethoven died 1827).

**Range:** C4–G4. Stays on D and A strings in first position. Perfect for a
beginner violin arrangement in D major.

**Fingering summary (D major, first position):**
- C natural on A string → 1st finger, low position (C♮, not C♯ — this note
  only appears as a passing tone; in D major the C would normally be C♯, but
  Beethoven uses C♮ for the Lydian touch)
- D: open D string
- E: 1st finger on D string
- F♯: 2nd finger on D string
- G: 3rd finger on D string or open G

**Practice tips:**
- Down-bow on every quarter note here — keep it marching.
- The famous four-note motif E-E-F-G repeats many times. Make each repetition
  sound fresh by varying your bow pressure slightly.
- The C♮ (low 1st finger on A string) in bar 9 is the only "tricky" note.
  Practice bar 9 alone: D E F E C → D.
