# Arkansas Traveler

**Provenance:** Traditional American fiddle tune, widely published by 1851.
Public domain.

**Range:** D4–F♯5. Climbs into second position momentarily (F♯5 on E string,
4th finger); alternatively play it as 1st finger E-string and stay in first
position.

**Why this is intermediate:**
- Faster tempo (♩=116) demands smooth string crossings.
- The B section uses a repeated falling figure (F♯–E–F♯–E) across two strings
  that requires precise bow distribution.

**Fiddle style notes:**
- In fiddle tradition: little to no vibrato; straight bowing; light, bouncy
  bow on the upbeats.
- The "choppy" fiddle articulation comes from playing short détaché strokes —
  not full-bow slurs.
- Accents on beats 1 and 3 give it the dance-hall drive.

**Practice tips:**
- Learn each 8-bar section separately before joining them.
- If the F♯ string crossing trips you up, slow to ♩=60 and isolate bars 1–2
  of the B section.
